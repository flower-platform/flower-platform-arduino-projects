Controlling lights with an Arduino is probably one of the first things that comes into our minds when we think about automating our home or office. Not necessarily because of its tremendous utility (?), but rather because it’s relatively simple to integrate into the existing legacy electrical system. I.e. there is no need to install actuators, motors, valves, sensors, etc. Only a nice relay would do a pretty good job.

#1 – switch lights on/off without leaving the comfortable position at desk

From our experience, the most interesting use case is at our office. We work in a building with several levels, each level being an open space. During the day, there is no need for artificial light. But as the sun goes down, somebody would need to physically go to the light switch to turn the lights on. I think everybody agrees that this is a physically demanding task, especially after a whole day of intense workout (i.e. fingers continuously hitting keyboards; and mouse too!)So now, there is no need to leave the comfortable position at the computer. Everybody can control the lights from a nice web interface, within the browser (desktop, tablet, mobile).

#2 – turn off lights for other levels, for the fun of it

A corollary of the above is that now everybody can switch lights on/off. E.g. for the colleagues that work at the level below. However, this works optimally from the mobile, while the author of the prank needs to be visiting the neighbors downstairs, in order to enjoy their reaction.
